US Inaugural Addresses Search Engine

About This Project

This project is a search engine specifically designed to explore the inaugural addresses of US presidents. By leveraging Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, it allows users to find relevant content within these historical speeches based on their search queries.

The search results are ranked based on their relevance, using a method called "cosine similarity." This measures how closely the content of a speech matches the user's query. The higher the similarity score, the more relevant the speech is considered to be.

This tool is particularly valuable for historians, researchers, and anyone interested in exploring the rhetoric and themes of US presidents over the years.
